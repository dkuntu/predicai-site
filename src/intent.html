The Intent Protocol

The cure for generic AI output. Build intent before you prompt.

The Problem: "The Lazy Prompt"

Most professionals treat AI like a search engine. They ask a vague question and hope for a specific answer. That creates a "Faster Mess."

The Trap

"Summarize this meeting."

Result: A generic wall of text that misses the actual business decision.

The Fix (Architecture)

"Act as a Chief of Staff. Extract only the revenue-driving decisions from this transcript..."

Result: A strategic asset.

The R.I.S.E. Framework

Do not hit "Enter" until you have defined these four elements.

R

Role

Who is the AI? Give it a job title. (e.g., "Senior Legal Counsel," "Python Engineer," "Chief of Staff").

I

Input & Context

What does it need to know? Paste the data, the background story, and the goal.

S

Steps

How should it think? Force it to work linearly. (e.g., "First, analyze X. Then, draft Y.")

E

Expectation

What is the output format? (e.g., "Bottom Line Up Front," "Table format," "No pleasantries").

What this unlocks:

Meetings: Transcript → Decisions & Actions
Emails: Rough notes → Executive-ready draft
Research: 50 pages → Verified summary + gaps
Planning: Goals → 30/60/90 Day Plan

The Intent Builder

Copy this template. Paste it into Claude or ChatGPT. Fill in the brackets.

(R) ROLE: Act as a [Insert Job Title]. Your superpower is [Insert Specific Skill, e.g., brevity, Python coding]. (I) INPUT & CONTEXT: Here is the situation: [Describe Context]. Here is the data: [Paste Data/Text]. My Goal is: [Define Success]. (S) STEPS: 1. First, analyze the input for [Specific Criteria]. 2. Then, identify [Key Risk or Opportunity]. 3. Finally, draft the response. (E) EXPECTATION: Format: [e.g., Bullet points, Table, Email draft]. Tone: [e.g., Professional, Urgent, Direct]. Constraints: [e.g., No fluff, under 200 words].
